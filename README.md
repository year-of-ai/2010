# The Year 2010

A self-growing knowledge base about **the year 2010** — history, science, technology, arts, society, and people.

This repository is both a knowledge base and a reusable framework. It expands autonomously via `/grow`, which researches new on-topic content, builds structure, verifies facts, and publishes — all driven by the Concept Definition in [`seed.md`](seed.md). To retarget the framework at a different subject, run `/genesis "<new concept>"`.

It is the successor instance in its lineage, spawned from [year-of-ai/2009](https://github.com/year-of-ai/2009) — the year 2009.

## Notable Events of 2010

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Deepwater Horizon oil spill](history-politics/deepwater-horizon-oil-spill.md) | The explosion of the BP Deepwater Horizon rig on April 20 triggered the largest marine oil spill in history, releasing approximately 4.9 million barrels into the Gulf of Mexico over 87 days. |
| [Haiti earthquake](history-politics/haiti-earthquake.md) | A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Haiti on January 12, killing an estimated 100,000–316,000 people and displacing 1.5 million, making it one of the deadliest natural disasters in recorded history. |
| Arab Spring begins | Mass protests and uprisings swept the Arab world starting in late 2010, beginning with Tunisia's Jasmine Revolution (December 17), setting off a wave of political change across the region. |
| Affordable Care Act signed | President Obama signed the Affordable Care Act on March 23, the most significant overhaul of the US healthcare system since Medicare and Medicaid were established in 1965. |
| Chilean mining rescue | All 33 miners trapped 700 meters underground at the San José Mine in Chile were rescued alive on October 13 after 69 days, watched live by an estimated 1 billion television viewers. |
| WikiLeaks releases diplomatic cables | WikiLeaks began publishing 251,287 classified US State Department diplomatic cables in November, exposing sensitive diplomatic communications in a major intelligence disclosure. |
| iPad launched | Apple released the iPad on April 3, establishing the modern tablet computer category and selling 15 million units in its first year, fundamentally changing personal computing. |
| FIFA World Cup in South Africa | The 2010 FIFA World Cup was held in South Africa from June 11 to July 11 — the first to be staged on the African continent — with Spain winning their first title. |
| Dodd-Frank Act signed | The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act was signed on July 21, enacting the most sweeping financial regulation reforms since the Great Depression in response to the 2008 crisis. |
| *The Social Network* released | David Fincher's film about Facebook's founding opened on October 1, winning 3 Academy Awards and grossing $225 million, crystallizing Silicon Valley entrepreneurship in popular culture. |
| Deepwater Horizon well capped | After 87 days flowing uncontrolled, the Macondo well was permanently sealed on September 19, concluding the longest and largest accidental marine oil spill in US history. |
| First exoplanet in habitable zone confirmed | NASA's Kepler mission confirmed Kepler-9b and Kepler-9c as the first multi-planet system around a sun-like star, advancing the search for Earth-like worlds. |
| Google announces withdrawal from China | Google announced on January 12 it would stop censoring search results in China, leading to its redirect to Hong Kong servers and heightening debate over internet censorship and corporate responsibility. |
