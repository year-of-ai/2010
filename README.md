## Notable Events of 2010

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Deepwater Horizon oil spill]({{ '/news/history-politics/deepwater-horizon-oil-spill/' | relative_url }}) | The explosion of the BP Deepwater Horizon rig on April 20 triggered the largest marine oil spill in history, releasing approximately 4.9 million barrels into the Gulf of Mexico over 87 days. |
| [Haiti earthquake]({{ '/news/history-politics/haiti-earthquake/' | relative_url }}) | A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Haiti on January 12, killing an estimated 100,000–316,000 people and displacing 1.5 million, making it one of the deadliest natural disasters in recorded history. |
| [Citizens United v. FEC]({{ '/news/history-politics/citizens-united-v-fec/' | relative_url }}) | The Supreme Court's January 21 decision struck down campaign finance restrictions, enabling unlimited political spending and reshaping American elections through the rise of Super PACs. |
| [Conan O'Brien's Tonight Show transition]({{ '/news/arts-culture/conan-obrien-tonight-show-transition/' | relative_url }}) | NBC abruptly ended Conan O'Brien's seven-month tenure as Tonight Show host on January 22, reinstating Jay Leno; the conflict became a public relations disaster for NBC and a defining moment for social media activism. |
| [Avatar becomes highest-grossing film]({{ '/news/arts-culture/avatar/' | relative_url }}) | James Cameron's sci-fi epic surpassed *Titanic* on January 25 to become the highest-grossing film of all time; its 3D presentation and premium pricing drove the modern 3D cinema revolution. |
| [3D cinema revolution]({{ '/news/arts-culture/3d-cinema-revolution/' | relative_url }}) | Avatar's success catalyzed rapid 3D technology adoption across theaters and studios; 3D films earned $1.3 billion in 2010 (threefold increase from 2008), establishing premium pricing that sustained the theatrical experience. |
| [2010 Winter Olympics (Vancouver)]({{ '/news/history-politics/2010-winter-olympics-vancouver/' | relative_url }}) | Canada hosted a record-breaking Winter Olympics (February 12–28), winning 14 gold medals as the first host nation to top the medal count, with the men's hockey final drawing 114 million viewers worldwide. |
| [Arab Spring begins]({{ '/news/history-politics/arab-spring-tunisia/' | relative_url }}) | Mass protests and uprisings swept the Arab world starting in late 2010, beginning with Tunisia's Jasmine Revolution (December 17), setting off a wave of political change across the region. |
| [Affordable Care Act signed]({{ '/news/society-economics/affordable-care-act/' | relative_url }}) | President Obama signed the Affordable Care Act on March 23, the most significant overhaul of the US healthcare system since Medicare and Medicaid were established in 1965. |
| [Chilean mining rescue]({{ '/news/history-politics/chilean-mining-rescue/' | relative_url }}) | All 33 miners trapped 700 meters underground at the San José Mine in Chile were rescued alive on October 13 after 69 days, watched live by an estimated 1 billion television viewers. |
| [2010 Thai political crisis]({{ '/news/history-politics/2010-thai-political-crisis/' | relative_url }}) | Thailand's Red Shirt pro-democracy movement occupied Bangkok for two months; a May 19 military crackdown killed 91+ people and wounded over 1,800, marking the bloodiest street confrontation in modern Thai history. |
| [2010 United Kingdom general election]({{ '/news/history-politics/2010-uk-general-election/' | relative_url }}) | Britain held its first hung parliament election since 1974 on May 6, returning 306 Conservative, 258 Labour, and 57 Lib Dem MPs; David Cameron and Nick Clegg formed the UK's first coalition since WWII. |
| [Flash Crash]({{ '/news/society-economics/flash-crash/' | relative_url }}) | On May 6, the Dow Jones fell nearly 1,000 points (9%) in 36 minutes before recovering, marking the market's first major algorithmic-trading catastrophe and triggering new regulatory circuit-breaker safeguards. |
| [Mavi Marmara flotilla raid]({{ '/news/history-politics/mavi-marmara-incident/' | relative_url }}) | Israeli naval commandos raided a Turkish humanitarian ship on May 31 in international waters, killing nine activists; the incident damaged Israel-Turkey relations and sparked international controversy over Gaza blockade and use of force. |
| [WikiLeaks releases diplomatic cables]({{ '/news/history-politics/wikileaks-cables/' | relative_url }}) | WikiLeaks began publishing 251,287 classified US State Department diplomatic cables in November, exposing sensitive diplomatic communications in a major intelligence disclosure. |
| [iPad launched]({{ '/news/science-technology/ipad-launch/' | relative_url }}) | Apple released the iPad on April 3, establishing the modern tablet computer category and selling 15 million units in its first year, fundamentally changing personal computing. |
| [2010 United States Census]({{ '/news/society-economics/2010-us-census/' | relative_url }}) | The April 1 Census recorded 308.7 million residents (9.7% growth since 2000) and revealed significant demographic shifts: the South and West gained congressional representation; Hispanic and Asian populations surged. |
| [Eyjafjallajökull eruption]({{ '/news/history-politics/eyjafjallajokull-eruption/' | relative_url }}) | Iceland's Eyjafjallajökull volcano erupted on April 14, sending ash across European airspace and triggering the largest air traffic closure since World War II, affecting 100,000+ flights. |
| [FIFA World Cup in South Africa]({{ '/news/history-politics/fifa-world-cup-2010/' | relative_url }}) | The 2010 FIFA World Cup was held in South Africa from June 11 to July 11 — the first to be staged on the African continent — with Spain winning their first title. |
| [*Toy Story 3* released]({{ '/news/arts-culture/toy-story-3/' | relative_url }}) | Pixar's animated sequel, released June 18, grossed $1.067 billion worldwide to become the highest-grossing film of 2010 and the first animated film to reach $1 billion. |
| [iPhone 4 launched]({{ '/news/science-technology/iphone-4-launch/' | relative_url }}) | Apple released the iPhone 4 on June 24 with a revolutionary Retina display (326 ppi), flat glass-and-steel design, dual cameras, and FaceTime video calling; 1.7 million units sold in first weekend. |
| [Dodd-Frank Act signed]({{ '/news/society-economics/dodd-frank-act/' | relative_url }}) | The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act was signed on July 21, enacting the most sweeping financial regulation reforms since the Great Depression in response to the 2008 crisis. |
| [*The Social Network* released]({{ '/news/arts-culture/the-social-network/' | relative_url }}) | David Fincher's film about Facebook's founding opened on October 1, winning 3 Academy Awards and grossing $225 million, crystallizing Silicon Valley entrepreneurship in popular culture. |
| [*Inception* released]({{ '/news/arts-culture/inception/' | relative_url }}) | Christopher Nolan's science-fiction thriller premiered on July 16 to critical acclaim and commercial success, grossing $839 million worldwide and winning 4 Academy Awards, exemplifying ambitious blockbuster filmmaking. |
| [The King's Speech released]({{ '/news/arts-culture/the-kings-speech/' | relative_url }}) | Tom Hooper's historical drama premiered September 6, earning $414 million worldwide and winning four Academy Awards, becoming the most successful independent British film ever released. |
| [Deepwater Horizon well capped]({{ '/news/history-politics/deepwater-horizon-macondo-well-capped/' | relative_url }}) | The Macondo well was permanently sealed on September 19 via a relief-well bottom kill, ending the largest accidental marine oil spill in history. |
| [Kepler-9 multi-planet system confirmed]({{ '/news/science-technology/kepler-9-exoplanet-discovery/' | relative_url }}) | NASA confirmed Kepler-9b and Kepler-9c on August 31, the first multiple planets seen transiting one sun-like star, pioneering transit-timing detection. |
| [Gliese 581g discovery]({{ '/news/science-technology/gliese-581g-exoplanet/' | relative_url }}) | On September 29, astronomers announced Gliese 581g, a potentially habitable exoplanet; the discovery thrilled the scientific community but was later refuted through rigorous peer review and analysis. |
| [Google announces withdrawal from China]({{ '/news/science-technology/google-china-withdrawal/' | relative_url }}) | Google announced on January 12 it would stop censoring search results in China, leading to its redirect to Hong Kong servers and heightening debate over internet censorship and corporate responsibility. |
| [Pakistan floods]({{ '/news/history-politics/pakistan-floods/' | relative_url }}) | Unprecedented monsoon flooding in July–August devastated Pakistan, affecting ~20 million people and demonstrating South Asia's vulnerability to climate-related natural disasters. |
| [Russia heat wave and wildfires]({{ '/news/history-politics/russia-heatwave-wildfires-2010/' | relative_url }}) | An unprecedented heat wave gripped western Russia from late July through September, with temperatures reaching 44°C; wildfires burned 300,000+ hectares and killed an estimated 55,000 people through combined heat and air pollution effects. |
| [Instagram launched]({{ '/news/science-technology/instagram-launch/' | relative_url }}) | Instagram, a photo-sharing app, launched on iOS on October 6, reaching 1 million users in two months and pioneering the mobile-first social media era with visual content focus. |
| [Mohamed Bouazizi — Arab Spring catalyst]({{ '/news/people/mohamed-bouazizi/' | relative_url }}) | Tunisian street vendor whose December 17 self-immolation protesting corruption and police abuse sparked the Jasmine Revolution, triggering the Arab Spring across the Middle East. |
| [Liu Xiaobo wins Nobel Peace Prize]({{ '/news/people/liu-xiaobo/' | relative_url }}) | The Chinese dissident and human-rights activist was awarded the 2010 Nobel Peace Prize on October 8 while imprisoned for Charter 08 authorship; he was the first ethnic Chinese to win while in China. |
| [Aung San Suu Kyi released from house arrest]({{ '/news/people/aung-san-suu-kyi/' | relative_url }}) | Myanmar's pro-democracy leader and Nobel laureate, detained roughly 15 years, was freed November 13, days after the junta's first multiparty elections in 20 years. |
| [Corazon Aquino's legacy and the 2010 election]({{ '/news/people/corazon-aquino/' | relative_url }}) | The People Power leader's 2009 death propelled her son Benigno Aquino III to win the May 10 Philippine presidential election, renewing her democratic legacy. |
| [Stuxnet cyberweapon discovered]({{ '/news/science-technology/stuxnet/' | relative_url }}) | A sophisticated worm targeting Siemens PLCs, discovered June 2010, became the first known malware to cause physical destruction to industrial infrastructure, sabotaging Iran's Natanz centrifuges. |
| [2010 Chile earthquake]({{ '/news/history-politics/2010-chile-earthquake/' | relative_url }}) | A magnitude 8.8 megathrust earthquake struck off Chile's Maule coast on February 27, killing 525 people and causing ~US$30 billion in damage — the sixth-strongest earthquake ever instrumentally recorded. |
| [Burj Khalifa opens]({{ '/news/science-technology/burj-khalifa/' | relative_url }}) | Dubai inaugurated the Burj Khalifa on January 4 as the world's tallest building at 828 metres, redefining the limits of skyscraper engineering. |
| [European sovereign debt crisis]({{ '/news/society-economics/european-sovereign-debt-crisis/' | relative_url }}) | Greece took a €110 billion EU–IMF bailout on May 2 and the eurozone built a €750 billion backstop, exposing the single currency's structural fragility. |
| [*Biutiful* premiered at Cannes]({{ '/news/arts-culture/biutiful/' | relative_url }}) | Alejandro González Iñárritu's Spanish-language drama premiered May 17 at Cannes, where Javier Bardem won Best Actor and earned the first Oscar Best Actor nomination for an all-Spanish-language role. |
| [Smolensk air disaster]({{ '/news/history-politics/smolensk-air-disaster/' | relative_url }}) | A Polish Air Force Tu-154 crashed near Smolensk on April 10, killing President Lech Kaczyński and 95 others, decimating Poland's political and military leadership. |
| [First synthetic cell (JCVI-syn1.0)]({{ '/news/science-technology/synthetic-cell-jcvi-syn1/' | relative_url }}) | The J. Craig Venter Institute announced JCVI-syn1.0 on May 20, the first cell run by a chemically synthesized genome, a milestone for synthetic biology. |
| [J. Craig Venter and synthetic biology leadership]({{ '/news/science-technology/craig-venter-synthetic-biology/' | relative_url }}) | Geneticist Venter led the JCVI team that unveiled JCVI-syn1.0 on May 20, establishing synthetic biology as an engineering discipline built on designed genomes. |
| [Microsoft Kinect launched]({{ '/news/science-technology/kinect-launch/' | relative_url }}) | Microsoft released the controller-free Kinect on November 4, setting a Guinness record as the fastest-selling consumer electronics device and bringing depth-sensing into mainstream homes. |
| [LeBron James' "The Decision"]({{ '/news/people/lebron-james-the-decision/' | relative_url }}) | LeBron James announced his move to the Miami Heat in a July 8 ESPN special, inaugurating the NBA's modern player-empowerment and superteam era. |
| [2010 US midterm elections]({{ '/news/history-politics/2010-us-midterm-elections/' | relative_url }}) | Republicans gained 63 House seats on November 2, securing control and ending Democratic majority; the Tea Party movement emerged as a defining force, reshaping the decade's political trajectory. |
| [Julian Assange and WikiLeaks]({{ '/news/people/julian-assange/' | relative_url }}) | WikiLeaks' November 28 release of 251,287 US State Department diplomatic cables brought founder Julian Assange to global prominence; his December 7 arrest began a decade-long legal battle. |
| [SpaceX Falcon 9/Dragon COTS Demo Flight 1]({{ '/news/science-technology/spacex-falcon-9-dragon-cots-demo-1/' | relative_url }}) | SpaceX achieved a milestone on December 8 when Falcon 9 launched Dragon spacecraft, becoming the first private company to launch, orbit, and recover a spacecraft, opening the commercial spaceflight era. |
| [Shanghai Expo 2010]({{ '/news/society-economics/shanghai-expo-2010/' | relative_url }}) | China's first World's Fair (May–October) drew a record 73 million visits, showcasing urban-sustainability innovation and cementing Shanghai as a global economic hub. |
| [Global jobless recovery]({{ '/news/society-economics/global-unemployment-and-economic-recovery-2010/' | relative_url }}) | Growth resumed but unemployment stayed high (US 9.6%, eurozone 10.1%, Spain 20%+), entrenching structural labor-market damage and fueling a decade of political polarization. |
| [Graphene and the 2010 Nobel Prize in Physics]({{ '/news/science-technology/graphene-nobel-prize-physics-2010/' | relative_url }}) | The October 5 Nobel Prize honored Andre Geim and Konstantin Novoselov for isolating graphene, launching the field of two-dimensional materials. |
| [Sinking of ROKS Cheonan]({{ '/news/history-politics/roks-cheonan-sinking/' | relative_url }}) | An international investigation concluded a North Korean torpedo sank the South Korean corvette Cheonan on March 26, killing 46 and collapsing inter-Korean relations. |
| [J. D. Salinger dies]({{ '/news/people/jd-salinger/' | relative_url }}) | J. D. Salinger, reclusive author of The Catcher in the Rye, died January 27 at 91, ending one of American literature's most singular careers. |
| [Lionel Messi wins the first FIFA Ballon d'Or]({{ '/news/people/lionel-messi-ballon-dor-2010/' | relative_url }}) | Messi won the first merged FIFA Ballon d'Or for his 2010 season, topping an all-Barcelona podium that crowned Guardiola's tiki-taka era. |
| [Nelson Mandela's 92nd birthday and first Mandela Day]({{ '/news/people/nelson-mandela-92nd-birthday/' | relative_url }}) | Mandela turned 92 on July 18, the first UN-designated Nelson Mandela International Day, institutionalizing his legacy through the global 67-minutes service pledge. |
| [Twitter and social media growth]({{ '/news/society-economics/twitter-social-media-growth-2010/' | relative_url }}) | Twitter scaled to roughly 90 million tweets a day in 2010, emerging as real-time news infrastructure and launching its Promoted Tweets ad model. |
| [LHC achieves first 7 TeV collisions]({{ '/news/science-technology/large-hadron-collider-7-tev-collisions/' | relative_url }}) | On March 30, CERN's Large Hadron Collider reached a record 7 TeV, launching the physics run that discovered the Higgs boson in 2012. |
