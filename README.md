## Notable Events of 2010

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Deepwater Horizon oil spill](history-politics/deepwater-horizon-oil-spill.md) | The explosion of the BP Deepwater Horizon rig on April 20 triggered the largest marine oil spill in history, releasing approximately 4.9 million barrels into the Gulf of Mexico over 87 days. |
| [Haiti earthquake](history-politics/haiti-earthquake.md) | A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Haiti on January 12, killing an estimated 100,000–316,000 people and displacing 1.5 million, making it one of the deadliest natural disasters in recorded history. |
| [Arab Spring begins](history-politics/arab-spring-tunisia.md) | Mass protests and uprisings swept the Arab world starting in late 2010, beginning with Tunisia's Jasmine Revolution (December 17), setting off a wave of political change across the region. |
| [Affordable Care Act signed](society-economics/affordable-care-act.md) | President Obama signed the Affordable Care Act on March 23, the most significant overhaul of the US healthcare system since Medicare and Medicaid were established in 1965. |
| [Chilean mining rescue](history-politics/chilean-mining-rescue.md) | All 33 miners trapped 700 meters underground at the San José Mine in Chile were rescued alive on October 13 after 69 days, watched live by an estimated 1 billion television viewers. |
| [2010 Thai political crisis](history-politics/2010-thai-political-crisis.md) | Thailand's Red Shirt pro-democracy movement occupied Bangkok for two months; a May 19 military crackdown killed 91+ people and wounded over 1,800, marking the bloodiest street confrontation in modern Thai history. |
| [WikiLeaks releases diplomatic cables](history-politics/wikileaks-cables.md) | WikiLeaks began publishing 251,287 classified US State Department diplomatic cables in November, exposing sensitive diplomatic communications in a major intelligence disclosure. |
| [iPad launched](science-technology/ipad-launch.md) | Apple released the iPad on April 3, establishing the modern tablet computer category and selling 15 million units in its first year, fundamentally changing personal computing. |
| [Eyjafjallajökull eruption](history-politics/eyjafjallajokull-eruption.md) | Iceland's Eyjafjallajökull volcano erupted on April 14, sending ash across European airspace and triggering the largest air traffic closure since World War II, affecting 100,000+ flights. |
| [FIFA World Cup in South Africa](history-politics/fifa-world-cup-2010.md) | The 2010 FIFA World Cup was held in South Africa from June 11 to July 11 — the first to be staged on the African continent — with Spain winning their first title. |
| [Dodd-Frank Act signed](society-economics/dodd-frank-act.md) | The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act was signed on July 21, enacting the most sweeping financial regulation reforms since the Great Depression in response to the 2008 crisis. |
| [*The Social Network* released](arts-culture/the-social-network.md) | David Fincher's film about Facebook's founding opened on October 1, winning 3 Academy Awards and grossing $225 million, crystallizing Silicon Valley entrepreneurship in popular culture. |
| [*Inception* released](arts-culture/inception.md) | Christopher Nolan's science-fiction thriller premiered on July 16 to critical acclaim and commercial success, grossing $839 million worldwide and winning 4 Academy Awards, exemplifying ambitious blockbuster filmmaking. |
| [The King's Speech released](arts-culture/the-kings-speech.md) | Tom Hooper's historical drama premiered September 6, earning $414 million worldwide and winning four Academy Awards, becoming the most successful independent British film ever released. |
| [Deepwater Horizon well capped](history-politics/deepwater-horizon-macondo-well-capped.md) | The Macondo well was permanently sealed on September 19 via a relief-well bottom kill, ending the largest accidental marine oil spill in history. |
| [Kepler-9 multi-planet system confirmed](science-technology/kepler-9-exoplanet-discovery.md) | NASA confirmed Kepler-9b and Kepler-9c on August 31, the first multiple planets seen transiting one sun-like star, pioneering transit-timing detection. |
| [Gliese 581g discovery](science-technology/gliese-581g-exoplanet.md) | On September 29, astronomers announced Gliese 581g, a potentially habitable exoplanet; the discovery thrilled the scientific community but was later refuted through rigorous peer review and analysis. |
| [Google announces withdrawal from China](science-technology/google-china-withdrawal.md) | Google announced on January 12 it would stop censoring search results in China, leading to its redirect to Hong Kong servers and heightening debate over internet censorship and corporate responsibility. |
| [Pakistan floods](history-politics/pakistan-floods.md) | Unprecedented monsoon flooding in July–August devastated Pakistan, affecting ~20 million people and demonstrating South Asia's vulnerability to climate-related natural disasters. |
| [Instagram launched](science-technology/instagram-launch.md) | Instagram, a photo-sharing app, launched on iOS on October 6, reaching 1 million users in two months and pioneering the mobile-first social media era with visual content focus. |
| [Mohamed Bouazizi — Arab Spring catalyst](people/mohamed-bouazizi.md) | Tunisian street vendor whose December 17 self-immolation protesting corruption and police abuse sparked the Jasmine Revolution, triggering the Arab Spring across the Middle East. |
| [Aung San Suu Kyi released from house arrest](people/aung-san-suu-kyi.md) | Myanmar's pro-democracy leader and Nobel laureate, detained roughly 15 years, was freed November 13, days after the junta's first multiparty elections in 20 years. |
| [Corazon Aquino's legacy and the 2010 election](people/corazon-aquino.md) | The People Power leader's 2009 death propelled her son Benigno Aquino III to win the May 10 Philippine presidential election, renewing her democratic legacy. |
